Commentary on Article 429 — Statute of Limitations for Contractual Disputes
Vietnamese Civil Code 2015 (Law No. 91/2015/QH13)

OVERVIEW
Article 429 establishes a three-year statute of limitations for filing lawsuits
related to contractual disputes. This article is part of Chapter XV (Common
Provisions on Contracts) under Part Three (Civil Obligations and Contracts).

KEY POINTS

1. Duration: Three (3) years from the triggering date.

2. Triggering date: The date on which the entitled person "knew or should have
   known" that their lawful rights and interests were infringed.

3. Scope: Applies to all contractual disputes governed by the Civil Code,
   including sales contracts, service contracts, lease contracts, and loan
   contracts.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS

- Filing deadline calculation: If a breach of contract occurs on January 15, 2024,
  the injured party has until January 15, 2027 to file suit.

- "Should have known" standard: The court may determine that a party "should
  have known" of a breach even if they did not actually discover it, provided a
  reasonable person in similar circumstances would have discovered it.

- Tolling and suspension: The statute of limitations may be suspended during
  periods of force majeure (Article 156) or when the entitled person is a minor
  without a legal representative.

RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER ARTICLES

- Article 351: General liability for breach of civil obligations
- Article 352: Compensation for damages due to breach
- Article 360: Compensation for damage due to breach of contract
- Article 419: Specific rules on contractual damage compensation
- Article 420: Penalty clauses for breach of contract
- Article 421: Exemptions from liability (force majeure, fault of aggrieved party)
- Article 468: Interest rate caps (20% per annum ceiling)

CASE LAW EXAMPLES

1. Supreme Court Resolution No. 01/2005: Clarified that the limitation period
   begins when the breach causes actual damage, not when the contract was signed.

2. Judgment No. 35/2020/KDTM: Held that continuous performance obligations
   restart the limitation period for each missed payment or delivery.

3. Provincial Court of Ho Chi Minh City (2021): Ruled that email notice of
   default constitutes sufficient evidence of "knew or should have known" date.

COMPARISON WITH COMMERCIAL LAW

Under the Law on Commerce (2005), Article 319 prescribes a shorter two-year
limitation period for commercial disputes. Practitioners should determine
whether the dispute is commercial or civil in nature to apply the correct
statute of limitations.
