Vietnamese Civil Code 2015 — Contract Liability

Article 351 — Liability for breach of civil obligations

1. A party that fails to perform or improperly performs a civil obligation shall bear civil liability as prescribed in Article 352 of this Code.

2. In the case where a party fails to perform an obligation to pay money, such party must pay interest on the overdue amount at the rate agreed upon by the parties but not exceeding the ceiling rate prescribed by law during the period of default; if there is no agreement, the rate prescribed in Article 468(2) of this Code shall apply.

Article 352 — Liability for damages due to breach of obligation

A party that causes damage through breach of a civil obligation shall compensate for all the damage, unless otherwise agreed or prescribed by law. Compensation for damage shall be as prescribed in Articles 360, 361 and 362 of this Code.

Article 360 — Compensation for damage due to breach of contract

In the case of breach of an obligation under a contract, the aggrieved party may claim compensation for damage to the interests that it would have derived from performance of the contractual obligation. The aggrieved party must prove the damage.

Article 419 — Compensation for damage due to breach of contract

1. Compensation for damage arising from breach of contract includes material loss and spiritual loss actually suffered by the aggrieved party. The amount of compensation shall include the lost benefits that the aggrieved party could have earned if the breach had not occurred.

2. The party claiming compensation shall prove the actual loss and the extent of the loss, and the causal link between the breach and the loss.

3. The aggrieved party shall take reasonable measures to mitigate the loss. If the aggrieved party fails to do so, the breaching party may request a reduction of the compensation corresponding to the loss that could have been mitigated.

Article 420 — Penalty for breach of contract

1. The parties may agree on a penalty for breach of contract, under which the breaching party must pay a penalty to the aggrieved party.

2. The penalty level shall be agreed upon by the parties.

3. The parties may agree that the breaching party shall pay only the penalty but not the compensation, or pay both the penalty and the compensation. In the absence of an agreement on the relationship between the penalty and the compensation, the aggrieved party may claim both.

Article 421 — Exemption from civil liability for breach of contract

1. A party shall be exempt from civil liability for breach of contract in the following cases:

  • a) An event of force majeure occurs, unless otherwise agreed or prescribed by law;
  • b) The breach is entirely due to the fault of the aggrieved party;
  • c) The breach results from the implementation of a decision of a competent state agency that the parties could not have been aware of at the time of entering into the contract.

2. A party exempted from civil liability for breach of contract must promptly notify the other party of the grounds for exemption.

Article 429 — Statute of limitations for contractual disputes

The statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit for contractual disputes shall be three (3) years from the date on which the entitled person knew or should have known that his/her lawful rights and interests were infringed.

Article 468 — Interest rate

1. Interest rate in a loan shall be agreed upon by the parties.

2. Where the interest rate is agreed upon, it must not exceed 20% per annum of the loan amount, unless otherwise prescribed by law. Where the parties agree on a rate exceeding 20%, the excess rate shall be void.

3. Where interest-bearing is agreed but the rate is not specified or there is a dispute about the rate, the rate shall be 50% of the ceiling rate prescribed in paragraph 1 of this Article at the time of payment.
