# Cook County Circuit Court — Family Law Division Rules

## Rule 14.1: Filing Requirements for Family Law Motions

All motions filed in the Family Law Division of the Circuit Court of Cook County must comply with the following requirements:

1. **Format**: Motions shall be typed or printed on 8.5" × 11" paper, double-spaced, with margins of at least one inch on all sides.
2. **Caption**: Each motion must include the case number, division number, and the names of all parties.
3. **Certificate of Service**: A certificate of service must be attached to every motion, certifying that copies were served on all parties or their attorneys of record.
4. **E-Filing**: Effective January 1, 2018, all motions must be filed electronically through the Odyssey eFileIL system. Paper filings are accepted only upon showing of good cause.

## Rule 14.2: Notice Requirements

A party filing a motion must provide notice to all other parties at least seven (7) court days before the hearing date. Notice must include:

- The date, time, and courtroom of the hearing
- A copy of the motion and all supporting documents
- A proposed order

Emergency motions may be heard on shortened notice or without notice, but the moving party must demonstrate immediate and irreparable harm.

## Rule 14.3: Response Deadlines

Responses to motions must be filed within **thirty (30) days** of service. The response period may be extended by:

- Agreement of the parties (up to 14 additional days)
- Court order upon showing of good cause

Late-filed responses may be stricken at the court's discretion. All responses must include proof of service and must be filed through the e-filing system.

## Rule 14.4: Discovery in Family Law Cases

Discovery shall be conducted in accordance with Illinois Supreme Court Rules 201 through 214, with the following modifications:

1. **Initial Disclosures**: Within 60 days of the first case management conference, each party must disclose:
   - All sources of income for the preceding three years
   - A complete list of assets and liabilities
   - Health insurance coverage for all family members
   - Any existing court orders affecting the parties or children

2. **Financial Affidavits**: Each party must file a Financial Affidavit (Form CCG 1000) within 30 days of the initial case management conference.

3. **Depositions**: No more than three depositions per side without leave of court.

## Rule 14.5: Child Custody and Parenting Time

In cases involving allocation of parental responsibilities:

1. **Parenting Plan**: Each party must file a proposed parenting plan within 120 days of service of the petition.
2. **Mediation**: The court may order mediation before any contested hearing on parenting issues. The mediator's fees shall be allocated by the court.
3. **Guardian ad Litem**: The court may appoint a guardian ad litem to represent the best interests of the child. The GAL's fees are subject to allocation between the parties.
4. **Home Study**: Either party may request a home study, which shall be conducted by a licensed clinical social worker approved by the court.

## Rule 14.6: Maintenance (Alimony) Guidelines

Maintenance calculations shall follow the Illinois Marriage and Dissolution of Marriage Act (750 ILCS 5/504):

- **Duration**: For marriages of 5–20 years, maintenance duration equals (years of marriage × 0.33) years.
- **Amount**: Guideline maintenance = (33.33% of payor's net income) minus (25% of payee's net income), not to exceed 40% of combined net income.
- **Review**: Maintenance orders are subject to review every 3 years unless the court orders otherwise.

## Rule 14.7: Attorney Fee Petitions

Petitions for attorney fees must include:

1. A detailed billing statement showing dates, descriptions, and time spent
2. The attorney's hourly rate and qualifications
3. An affidavit of the reasonableness of fees
4. Documentation of the parties' respective financial positions

The court will consider the factors set forth in In re Marriage of Schneider, 214 Ill. 2d 152 (2005), including the relative financial resources of the parties and the complexity of the case.
